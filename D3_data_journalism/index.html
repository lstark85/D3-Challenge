Correlations Discovered Between Health Risks, Age, and Income

Household Income plays a role with health risks. For the majority of lower income households, there is a higher rate for obesity and smokers. However it is interesting to see that lack of healthcare remains fairly consistent despite the median income.

It is also interesting to see how Poverty plays a role with health risks. Those who are in poverty range consistently for obesity. About 12% and more are smokers. Also, those that are in poverty have between 6 and 20% that lack healthcare.

Finally, how does age become a factor for health risks? The median age ranges between 33 and 40 years of age. The obesity rate is between 22% or higher in this age group. With 13% or higher being smokers.
